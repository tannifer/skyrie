The Band

Lesley Thompson

An adopted Scot, Lesley has performed solo and with several bands including Hurricane Road, The Lomond Ceilidh Band and Freereelin’, and has worked as a session musician in various genres: Folk, Bluegrass, Trad and Gypsy. She also teaches, composes Scottish fiddle tunes, and has competed at the highest level in Scots fiddle, featuring as a Glenfiddich finalist for 3 years 2011-2014 and being invited to championships across Scotland. She also plays piano, bagpipes, whistle, mandolin and guitar.

Hannah Coutts

Hannah grew up learning fiddle in the pubs of Liverpool before relocating to Scotland at 18. Her playing has absorbed the best of Scottish, Irish and English influences to create a rhythmic sound filled with exciting harmonies and infectious enthusiasm. Hannah has played with numerous bands in a range of genres, from pure traditional folk to folk rock and heavy metal.

Paul Campbell

Paul has been playing locally around Dundee for 30 years. He has recorded with several artists most notably with the band Fon a Bhord (including Barbara Dymock, Helen Forbes Ian Kinnear and Mike Ward). Paul teaches guitar and is also is a tutor with the Tayside Young Fiddlers. He will be found most Thursday nights in the Fisherman’s session in Broughty Ferry.

Chris Smith

Chris spent 15 years as a session drummer recording, performing and touring with many artists including: Big Country, Nazareth, Dougie Maclean, Kathy Mattea, The Platters, Maggie Riley and Martin Taylor.

Chris also worked as a composer of neo-classical music and his work has featured on tv channels: Classic FM TV, o Music, C Music, the Performance Channel, Al Jazeera and CNN. Chris collaborated with Emmy Award winning Sex and the City producer Michael King.

Contemporary Ceilidh

Our standard line-up is: 2 Fiddles, Guitar and Drums. By request we also play highland pipes, flute, whistle, cittern and mandolin. Our ceilidh set consists of traditional and contemporary Scottish and Irish tunes, and some we’ve written ourselves. If in addition you have any special requests, we are always happy to oblige!

We are all confident callers, so whatever the line-up, we’ll be able to guide you through the dances. We call all the dances and demonstrate them; it is all part of the fun! We play all your favourite ceilidh dances, and add a few surprises.

  • Gay Gordons
  • Canadian Barn Dance (ordinary and progressive)
  • Heel Toe Polka
  • Dashing White Sergeant
  • Virginia Reel
  • Circassian Circle
  • Eightsome Reel
  • Strip the Willow
  • Orcadian Strip the Willow
  • Flying Scotsman
  • Military Twostep
  • St Bernard’s Waltz
  • Pride of Erin Waltz
  • Scottish Waltz
  • Highland Schottische
